I work on the calibrated permissioning frontier of biological AI safeguards: jointly minimizing false negatives (harmful assistance) and false positives (blocked legitimate research). My empirical finding (April 2026) is that no measured frontier model sits in the low-FPR / low-FNR region for biology-specific distributions. The portfolio below is built to close that gap.

Empirical calibration gap (April 2026)

Strict false-positive refusal rate (FPR) on 181 legitimate biology research queries (Tiers 1–4). Every refusal here is on a query a credentialed biology researcher would consider appropriate to answer.

Headline finding

Anthropic Sonnet 4.5/4.6 refused 33.7% of legitimate queries; Opus 4.7 refused 43.6%; the other 6 frontier models tested refused 0–0.5%. All Anthropic refusals were hard API-level stops (stop_reason="refusal", empty response). McNemar p<0.0001; exact binomial test vs published 0.05% baseline p<0.0001.
